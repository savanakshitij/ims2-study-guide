🔥 Materials — first up (3 Jul)
Two halves: Metals (Wettlaufer) — metallurgy, heat treatment, iron-carbon & TTT diagrams, deformation, testing/failure. Plastics (Gleiter/Gramlich) — polymer types, viscoelasticity/creep, thermal analysis (DSC), thermosets & elastomers, fibre-reinforced composites, rheology, and processing (injection moulding, extrusion). Practise reading the phase diagrams.
⚙️ Mechanics = two separate modules
EM2 (von Tardy-Tuch) is elastostatics — stress/strain, Mohr's circle, bending, moment of inertia, deflection curves. EM3 (Hufnagel) is kinematics & dynamics — instantaneous pole, impulse/momentum, energy methods. Different maths, study them as two exams.
💻 Programming 2 is C, not Java
Recursion, pointers, strings, structs, and linked lists. malloc/free and pointer arithmetic trip people up — write and trace actual code. The chained-lists exercises (insert/delete/sort, single & double) mirror likely exam tasks.
⚡ Electrical — field theory heavy
From past papers: electrostatics, capacitors, magnetic fields, Lorentz force, magnetic circuits, self-induction, AC power (single & three-phase), resonant circuits. Lab experiments (capacitors, rectifiers, AC, op-amp) can show up in writing too.
😬 Electrical → Maths back-to-back
22nd then 23rd is tight. Get Electrical solid early so the night between is a Maths 2 refresh, not first-time learning. You have SS23/SS24 Maths past papers with solutions — use them as mock exams.
∫ Maths 2 — only 4 chapters
Differential equations (1st & 2nd order, separation, exponential approach), Laplace transform, functions of several variables (partial derivatives, gradient, extrema, Hessian), multiple integrals (polar/spherical, area & volume). Tight scope — master all four.
